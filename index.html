Paving the Dirt Road: How LeBron James Turned a Hard Past Into a Future for Akron's Children

March 2025 5 min read By Off Court Insider

Wally Skalij / Los Angeles Times

Akron is a tough place to grow up. Once a thriving manufacturing hub, the city has faced decades of economic decline, poverty, and instability. For many families, resources are scarce and opportunity feels out of reach. LeBron James knows this firsthand. Raised by a single mother in some of the city's most difficult neighborhoods, he experienced housing instability, missed school days, and the constant uncertainty that comes with growing up without a financial safety net.

"I'm LeBron James, from Akron, Ohio. I'm not even supposed to be here."

LeBron James, after winning the 2013 NBA Finals with the Miami Heat

Those words said everything. LeBron didn't come from wealth, connections, or a clear path forward. He came from one of the hardest places in Ohio, raised on instability and uncertainty, with the odds stacked firmly against him. That he made it out at all was remarkable. That he became one of the greatest athletes in the history of his sport was the impossible made real. But what defines LeBron James as much as anything he has done on the court is what he chose to do once he got there. He turned around. He looked back at Akron and decided that his story, as unlikely as it was, did not have to be the exception. With the right support, the right resources, and someone willing to believe in them, more kids from hard places could make it out too.

Basketball eventually became his ticket out, but rather than simply leave, LeBron chose to look back. He recognized that his story wasn't unique, that thousands of Akron kids were walking the same hard road he once walked, and that with the right resources, they too could unlock their full potential. He didn't just want to write a check and walk away. He wanted to build something real, something rooted in the community he came from, a place where the kids who reminded him of his younger self would have the doors opened for them that were never opened for him. That conviction led him to put his name, his platform, and the full weight of the LeBron James Family Foundation behind the I Promise School, a commitment that has since grown into one of the most ambitious philanthropic efforts ever undertaken by a professional athlete.

In 2018, in partnership with Akron Public Schools, LeBron opened the I Promise School, a public school designed specifically for students who had fallen behind academically. Rather than writing these children off, IPS meets them where they are. Students benefit from a specialized curriculum, extended school days, and a culture built around support rather than shame. Even practical barriers are addressed head-on: students receive free uniforms and bicycles, ensuring that getting to school and being ready to learn is never an obstacle a child has to face alone.

LeBron and the LeBron James Family Foundation understood early on that a great classroom means nothing if a child has no stable home to return to. The I Promise Village, developed in partnership with Graduate Hotels, addresses this reality directly. The facility provides transitional housing to families of IPS students who are experiencing homelessness or extreme housing instability, giving children a safe, supportive environment where they can focus on their education rather than survival. The Village is not simply a roof over families' heads. It is a community. Residents have access to a community garden for fresh produce, a study center, an outdoor play area, and communal meals. The facility is staffed around the clock, with a house matriarch known lovingly as "Mama S" overseeing residents and providing the kind of nurturing, stable presence that many of these families have never had.

One of the most thoughtful aspects of the initiative is its understanding that children don't exist in isolation. A student's ability to thrive in school is deeply tied to the wellbeing of their entire family. That is why the I Promise ecosystem offers a robust set of wraparound services: an on-site food pantry, free legal assistance for families navigating housing or legal challenges, and GED and job training programs for parents. When a parent gains employment or achieves their own education goals, the ripple effect on their children is immeasurable. The network has since expanded further. "House Three Thirty" provides job training for the broader Akron community, while "I Promise HealthQuarters" delivers medical services, because good health is also a prerequisite for learning and growing.

Perhaps the most powerful commitment in the entire program is the simplest: every I Promise student is guaranteed tuition to the University of Akron. For a child raised in poverty, that promise removes one of the most daunting barriers to higher education and sends a message that someone believes in their future, unconditionally. In total, the LeBron James Family Foundation has invested over $41 million in these programs. But as significant as that number is, the real measure of impact is harder to quantify. It is the child who comes to school fed and rested because their family finally has a stable home. It is the parent who earns their GED and lands a steady job. It is the Akron kid who grows up knowing that their city and one of its greatest sons refused to give up on them.

The bravest fighter at UFC 311 never threw a punch

April 20264 min readBy Off Court Insider

Photo by Zac Pacleb/Zuffa LLC

Jiří Procházka has never seen the walls of the octagon as the boundary of his purpose. When the UFC light heavyweight champion learned of Ashly McGarity, a young girl courageously battling cervical cancer, he didn't just send his well wishes. He flew her out to the Intuit Dome in Inglewood, California to be by his side at UFC 311, because he wanted her to know she wasn't fighting alone.

For Procházka, it was personal. Before the fight, he shaved his head, not as a statement, but as an act of empathy. A quiet way of saying: I see you. I'm with you.

His commitment went further. Procházka donated $50,000 toward Ashly's chemotherapy treatment and surgery, hoping to ease a burden that no young person should have to carry. UFC CEO Dana White, moved by the gesture, matched the donation in full, bringing the total to $100,000 and amplifying a message Procházka has been determined to spread: that cancer patients deserve visibility, support, and hope.

As of late 2025, Ashly continues her recovery, pressing forward through ongoing chemotherapy with the same quiet bravery that stopped a champion in his tracks. Procházka has spoken openly about how deeply her story moved him, how a young girl facing something so devastating with such grace reminded him what real strength looks like. In trying to inspire her, she ended up inspiring him.
